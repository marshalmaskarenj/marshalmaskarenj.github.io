The files in the tutorial can be downloaded here.

Inside the downloaded .zip file, are two folders named 'OWL-18Oct' and 'case'.

Inside 'OWL-18Oct', are the files of Rhino Geometry and Grasshopper script. The .ghuser files for the grasshopper components are also inside a folder.

Inside 'case', there is the weather file that needs to be linked to the grasshopper script; although users could choose alternative EPW file as well.

Also, under a folder named 'masks' are the .mat files needed by the grasshopper script. Users need to connect location of 'case' to the workflow, ensuring that case/masks contains the four appropriate files.

Occupant Wellbeing through Lighting

OWL is developed by Marshal Maskarenj at LAB/LOCI UCLouvain, along with Bertrand Deroisy and Sergio Altomonte; as part of the FNRS funded project SCALE (Shading Control Algorithms for Luminance Evaluation).

OWL is an opensource workflow in Rhino Grasshopper developed for performing spectral lighting simulations, and evaluating non-image-forming effects of light on occupant wellbeing.
