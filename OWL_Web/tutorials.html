Tutorials

Listed below are tutorials that help install OWL on your system.

While the entry barrier on the hardware side is not too high, it is assumed you have Rhino 6 or 7 installed on your system, and have a working knowlege of Rhino viewports and Grasshopper canvas.

Tutorials 1 through 3 focus on installing prerequisites for OWL on your system: Python 2.7 and packages, Radiance, Ladybug 1 for imageviewer, and GH_Cpython.

Tutorial 4 presents how OWL components can be installed on your system, while Tutorial videos 5 and 6 give insights into a test case and on changing input parameters of the test case

Occupant Wellbeing through Lighting

OWL is developed by Marshal Maskarenj at LAB/LOCI UCLouvain, along with Bertrand Deroisy and Sergio Altomonte; as part of the FNRS funded project SCALE (Shading Control Algorithms for Luminance Evaluation).

OWL is an opensource workflow in Rhino Grasshopper developed for performing spectral lighting simulations, and evaluating non-image-forming effects of light on occupant wellbeing.
